How it works

From brakes-off to invoice, automatically

The AutoLog detects each flight, and the platform turns it into tech log entries, billing lines and maintenance hours before the pilot has left the apron.

1

Fly as normal

The AutoLog remains hidden and detects take-off and landing by itself. Pilots don't touch it. Many don't even know it's there.

2

Times upload themselves

After shutdown, flight and block times upload over the worldwide-supported 4G network, accurate to ±10 seconds.

3

Records fill in

Tech logs, flight records and aircraft hours populate automatically in the online platform.

4

Billing follows

Time-based invoices are generated from the recorded flights. No rounding, no arguments, no day of admin.

One system, two parts

The hardware and the platform

AutoLog, the invisible record keeper

A compact flight data recorder installed by a licensed engineer in low single-digit hours. It detects flights automatically, records flight, block and engine times, and uploads them after every shutdown. A minor installation on UK, EU and US-registered fixed- and rotary-wing aircraft.

Everything a flying school, club or group needs to run the business end-to-end: bookings, electronic tech logs, invoicing, student records, maintenance tracking and airfield movements, in one system that works as well on a phone at the aircraft as it does in the office.
