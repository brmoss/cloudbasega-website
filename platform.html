Bookings calendar

Aircraft and instructors on one timeline

Members book aircraft and instructors together on a shared club calendar, from a desktop in the office or a phone at the airfield. Maintenance blocks the aircraft automatically, and the calendar prevents double-booking.

  • Day, week and month views with drag-and-drop booking
  • Book an aircraft and instructor together, down to 15-minute slots
  • Maintenance and unavailability appear on the same timeline
  • Cancellation windows and permissions your club controls

eTechLog

The paper tech log, retired

A fully paperless technical log for every aircraft, in the same system as the bookings. Hours arrive from the AutoLog by themselves; pilots sign off on any device.

  • Daily check sign-off gates flying until the aircraft is inspected
  • Pre-flight and post-flight entries with PIC sign-off
  • Flight times, oil, fuel, landings and defects in one record
  • Complete, timestamped history for every aircraft

Invoicing

Billing that does itself

The flights recorded by the AutoLog are the flights on the invoice: same system, same numbers. Clubs that used to spend a day a month on billing now raise invoices in minutes, and pilots can pay before they've left the airfield.

  • Invoices generated from recorded flight times, ending rounding disputes
  • Multiple charge rates per aircraft and per member
  • Fuel reimbursement and VAT income reports handled
  • Online payments supported, so members can pay immediately

Student records

Competency-based training records

Every lesson is logged against the syllabus, in the same system as the booking and the tech log. Instructors sign off exercises with comments, and the student's hours build themselves from AutoLog-recorded flights.

  • Customisable courses: PPL, LAPL or your own syllabus
  • Exercises signed off per lesson, with instructor comments
  • Dual and solo hours accumulate automatically from recorded flights
  • Ground exam results and validity dates in the same record

Maintenance

Your engineer, in the loop by design

Maintenance doesn't live in a separate spreadsheet. Your engineer joins the school, club or group with the dedicated Engineer role: defect notifications arrive as they're raised, ready to defer or action, and hours to next check can be monitored remotely.

  • Add your maintenance engineer to the group as an Engineer
  • Defects reported in seconds from a phone at the aircraft, graded Minor, Be aware or Serious
  • Defect notifications to defer or to action, with the reporter's details attached
  • Hours to next check monitored remotely, fed by AutoLog-recorded flights
  • Airworthiness at a glance: renewals, next checks and open defects across the fleet
